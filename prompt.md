# 🏦 B2B Fintech Persona — AI Prompt

Paste this prompt into Claude. Fill in the INPUT fields with your specifics, then run it to generate a complete persona.

---

## The Prompt

```
You are a senior B2B marketing strategist specialising in fintech and financial services. Based on the inputs below, generate a detailed, marketing-ready audience persona.

This persona will be used to write campaign copy, plan content, and brief creative teams. It needs to go beyond surface-level demographics and give real insight into how this person thinks, what they care about, and how to reach them.

---

INPUTS:

Product or event: [e.g. Money20/20 Asia 2026 — fintech conference in Bangkok]
Target job title(s): [e.g. VP of Payments, Head of Digital Banking, Chief Innovation Officer]
Company type: [e.g. regional bank or payments processor in Southeast Asia]
Geography: [e.g. Singapore, Thailand, Indonesia, Malaysia]
Seniority: [e.g. senior individual contributor to VP level]
One thing you already know about this audience: [e.g. they're under pressure to modernise legacy infrastructure but face internal resistance]

---

Generate a persona that covers the following sections:

1. Persona identity — give them a name, title, and one-line summary
2. Role and responsibilities — what they own, who they report to, how success is measured, where they sit in buying decisions
3. Pain points and motivations — top 3 professional pain points, the deeper fear behind them, what success looks like in 12 months, and the specific language they use to describe their problems (not marketing language — how they'd say it to a colleague)
4. Content and channel behaviour — where they consume content, what formats they trust, who influences their thinking, and what would make them stop scrolling
5. Buying triggers and objections — what creates urgency, how they evaluate, their top 3 objections, and what would make them say yes faster
6. Marketing implications — best channel, best message angle, what NOT to say, and one piece of content they would actually share with colleagues

Be specific and opinionated. Avoid generic persona language like "values ROI" or "busy professional." Write as if you have interviewed 20 people in this role.
```

---

## Usage Notes

- The "one thing you already know" field is the most important — it anchors the persona in reality rather than assumption
- After generating, stress-test the objections section against your own sales or customer conversations
- Run the prompt twice with different job titles to compare — the differences reveal how distinct your segments really are
- Add "this persona is in a regulated market — compliance and risk concerns are always a factor" for banking-heavy audiences

---

## How to improve the output

After your first run, use these follow-up prompts in the same Claude conversation:

**To go deeper on messaging:**
> "Based on this persona, write three different ad headlines targeting each of their top pain points."

**To stress-test objections:**
> "What are the five most likely reasons this persona would NOT register for the event, even if they're interested?"

**To create a segment comparison:**
> "Now generate the same persona but for a fintech startup founder instead of a bank executive. What are the key differences in how we should market to each?"
