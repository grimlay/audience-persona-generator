# 📱 Consumer Fintech Persona — AI Prompt

Paste this prompt into Claude. Fill in the INPUT fields with your specifics, then run it to generate a complete persona.

---

## The Prompt

```
You are a senior consumer marketing strategist specialising in fintech and financial services. Based on the inputs below, generate a detailed, marketing-ready consumer persona.

This persona will be used to write campaign copy, plan content strategy, and brief creative teams. It needs to go beyond demographics and surface the emotional and behavioural reality of this person — how they think about money, what stops them from acting, and how to reach them authentically.

---

INPUTS:

Product: [e.g. a digital investment app targeting first-time retail investors in Singapore]
Target audience description: [e.g. working adults aged 25–35, earning SGD 4,000–8,000/month, starting to think seriously about investing but haven't started yet]
Geography: [e.g. Singapore]
Life stage: [e.g. early career, no dependents, renting]
One thing you already know about this audience: [e.g. they follow financial influencers on TikTok and Instagram but feel overwhelmed when they try to actually start investing]

---

Generate a persona that covers the following sections:

1. Persona identity — give them a name, age, job, and one-line summary of their relationship with money
2. Financial profile — what products they currently use, their financial literacy level, their primary financial goal, and how they emotionally relate to money
3. Pain points and motivations — top 3 financial pain points, the specific fear behind them, what they want to achieve in 12 months, and the language they actually use to describe their situation (not bank language — how they'd say it to a friend)
4. Content and channel behaviour — where they consume financial content, what formats they trust, who influences their financial decisions, and what would make them stop scrolling
5. Buying triggers and objections — what creates openness to trying a new product, how they evaluate it, their top 3 objections, and what reassures them
6. Marketing implications — best channel, best message angle, what NOT to say, and the one thing that would make them recommend the product to a friend

Be specific and emotionally intelligent. Avoid generic consumer persona language. Write as if you have done 20 in-depth customer interviews with this audience.
```

---

## Usage Notes

- "One thing you already know" is crucial — ground the persona in a real observation, not an assumption
- The "language they actually use" section is gold for copywriting — use these phrases directly in ad headlines and captions
- Consumer fintech personas are highly sensitive to trust signals; pay close attention to the objections section
- Add "this is a regulated financial product — we cannot promise returns or use certain claims" to shape compliant messaging angles

---

## How to improve the output

After your first run, use these follow-up prompts in the same Claude conversation:

**To get copy-ready language:**
> "Based on this persona, write five Instagram caption hooks that would stop this person from scrolling — using their own language, not financial jargon."

**To map the customer journey:**
> "Walk me through this persona's journey from first hearing about the product to making their first deposit — what touchpoints matter most and where do they drop off?"

**To stress-test the product fit:**
> "What are the three most likely reasons this persona would download the app but never deposit? What would fix each one?"
